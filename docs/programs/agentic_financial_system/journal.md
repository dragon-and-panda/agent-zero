# Agentic Financial System Mission Diary

This journal records decisions, experiments, and guardrail updates for the financial-system program.

---

## Entry 001 - Program Initialization

### Decisions
- Rejected private inbox scraping and email-list brokerage as non-compliant monetization tactics.
- Approved a legal-first portfolio built around:
  - Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service,
  - Public-Web Research Briefs,
  - Autonomous Listing Concierge,
  - later-stage educational content and templates.
- Deferred live trading and leveraged Forex activity until after stable operating revenue, cash reserves, and paper-tested risk controls exist.

### Why
- Consent and privacy failures create avoidable legal and reputational risk.
- Services that solve real customer workflow problems are easier to validate and automate.
- Diversified revenue lanes are more durable than a single speculative strategy.

### Immediate Next Steps
1. Score initial offers using the strategy instrument.
2. Write offer-specific SOPs and delivery checklists.
3. Identify the narrowest MVP for each approved lane.
4. Start collecting case-study material for future content products.

---

## Entry 002 - Operating Assumptions

### Assumptions
- Customers will pay for time savings, cleaner workflows, and better decisions faster than they will pay for abstract autonomy.
- Public-web research and listing optimization can be validated without privileged data access.
- Inbox processing is acceptable only when the mailbox owner directly authorizes access for their own benefit.

### Risks to Monitor
- Overbuilding automation before confirming demand.
- Taking on too many offers at once.
- Confusing "interesting" with "sellable."

### Controls
- New ideas must pass the scoring rubric.
- Each lane must define a clear buyer, deliverable, and proof-of-value metric before expansion.
