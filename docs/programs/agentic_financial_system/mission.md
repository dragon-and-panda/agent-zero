# Agentic Financial System — Mission Plan (Compliant)

This plan operationalizes the financial-system mission as a legal, ethical, and automatable program inside Agent Zero.

> Scope note: This program explicitly **does not** support harvesting or selling personal email lists without consent. All data handling must be permission-based and compliant with applicable laws/policies.

---

## 1) Program Objective

Build a self-sustaining online income system using autonomous agents, then allocate profits into disciplined capital growth workflows, while publishing transparent build logs and educational content.

Primary target outcomes:
- Generate repeatable online revenue from compliant digital ventures.
- Maintain a governed data pipeline for lead/contact intelligence.
- Reinvest profits into risk-managed trading research and execution.
- Keep a public-facing documentation/tutorial trail.

---

## 2) Safety, Compliance, and Ethics Guardrails

### 2.1 Non-negotiable Rules
1. No unauthorized scraping of private inboxes or third-party systems.
2. No sale of personal email lists or contact data without explicit consent and lawful basis.
3. No spam workflows; outreach must support opt-in + unsubscribe.
4. No guaranteed-profit claims in trading or public content.

### 2.2 Required Controls
- Data source registry with ownership + consent metadata.
- PII minimization (collect only fields required for intended use).
- Retention policy and deletion workflow.
- Audit trail for extraction, transformation, and export events.

---

## 3) Agentic Framework Design

### 3.1 Core Agent Roles
- **Apex Orchestrator:** Prioritizes missions and enforces guardrails.
- **Data Steward Agent:** Owns ingestion, consent checks, dedupe, and schema validation.
- **Monetization Analyst:** Tests compliant offer funnels and distribution channels.
- **Trading Research Agent:** Runs paper-trading strategies, risk reports, and post-trade analytics.
- **Content Producer Agent:** Converts operations into tutorials, scripts, and story arcs.
- **Finance Ops Agent:** Tracks revenue, expenses, transfers, and account reconciliation.

### 3.2 Operating Loop (Hourly/Daily)
1. Ingest approved data sources.
2. Validate compliance constraints.
3. Produce lead intelligence assets (not raw personal lists for resale).
4. Launch/optimize monetization experiments.
5. Allocate profits per risk policy.
6. Update journal, telemetry, and content pipeline.

---

## 4) Data Extraction & RAG Workflow (Google Email + Files)

### 4.1 Ingestion Scope
Permission-based extraction from:
- Inbox (received)
- Sent mail
- CC/BCC fields where accessible and lawful
- Attached or linked files approved for processing

### 4.2 Data Pipeline
1. Pull message metadata + bodies via approved API access.
2. Extract contact candidates (`from`, `to`, `cc`, signature blocks).
3. Normalize identities (lowercase, domain cleanup, dedupe).
4. Tag relationship/context (customer, partner, lead, vendor, unknown).
5. Build embeddings + index in RAG store for retrieval.
6. Store structured outputs in a governed contact intelligence table.

### 4.3 Orange DataScaping Usage
Use Orange DataScaping/Data Mining to:
- Profile contact/domain clusters.
- Detect duplicates and anomalies.
- Segment by engagement and business relevance.
- Export analysis-ready datasets for approved campaigns.

---

## 5) Monetization — Phase 1 (Compliant Alternatives)

Replace list-selling with legal revenue channels:
1. **Consent-based lead generation service**
   - Build segmented, opt-in prospect datasets for client campaigns.
2. **Newsletter/media asset monetization**
   - Sponsorships, affiliate placements, and paid placements.
3. **B2B research products**
   - Industry contact maps, trend briefs, and intent intelligence reports.
4. **Outbound-as-a-service (opt-in only)**
   - Campaign setup + copy + reporting for consenting audiences.

KPIs:
- Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
- Lead-to-conversion rate
- Cost per acquisition (CPA)
- Churn and retention

---

## 6) Financial Expansion — Post Phase 1

### 6.1 Capital Allocation Policy
- Stage 0: Emergency reserve and operating runway.
- Stage 1: Reinvest into business growth loops.
- Stage 2: Deploy capped trading capital.

### 6.2 Trading Roadmap (Start with Forex)
1. Strategy research + backtests.
2. Paper-trading validation with strict risk limits.
3. Small-cap live deployment after benchmark thresholds are met.
4. Continuous evaluation of Sharpe, drawdown, and expectancy.

Risk controls:
- Max daily and weekly drawdown caps
- Position sizing rules
- Hard stop-loss and trading halt triggers

---

## 7) Financial Management & Cash Movement

Finance Ops Agent responsibilities:
- Maintain ledger of all income streams and costs.
- Reconcile balances daily.
- Prepare transfer recommendations for deposits.
- Track transfers to designated destination account (e.g., `$Nicsins`) with manual verification checkpoints.

### 8.3 Narrative Layer (Anthropomorphic Character)

## 9) 30/60/90-Day Execution Milestones

### First 30 Days
- Stand up compliant ingestion + RAG pipeline.
- Launch first monetization experiment.
- Start daily journal + weekly report cadence.

### Day 31-60
- Scale winning channel(s), prune low ROI initiatives.
- Introduce paper-trading stack and analytics dashboard.
- Publish first tutorial module and pilot video script.

### Day 61-90
- Transition to systematic capital allocation.
- Run controlled live trading (if paper metrics pass thresholds).
- Release narrative-backed educational content series.

---

## 10) Definition of Done (Program Level)

Program is considered operational when:
- Compliance guardrails are enforced in every workflow.
- At least one repeatable, legal revenue engine is live.
- Trading operations remain risk-bounded and data-driven.
- Financial tracking + transfer workflow is auditable.
- Documentation/tutorial output is continuous and reusable.
