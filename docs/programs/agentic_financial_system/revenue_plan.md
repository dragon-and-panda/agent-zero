# Agentic Financial System Revenue Plan

This plan converts a broad autonomy-and-profit mission into concrete, lawful,
consent-based revenue lanes. The system should prioritize small, repeatable,
service-backed cash flows before moving into productized software or capital
deployment.

## Selection Filters
Every revenue lane must satisfy all of the following before activation:

1. **Legal:** No prohibited data acquisition, spam, credential misuse, or
   deceptive representations.
2. **Consent-based:** Customer-owned or explicitly opt-in data only.
3. **Auditable:** Inputs, outputs, prompts, and business actions can be logged.
4. **Low fixed cost:** Prefer workflows that can operate with lightweight
   infrastructure and clear unit economics.
5. **Short feedback loop:** The lane should produce measurable demand or delivery
   signals quickly.

## Priority Order
1. Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service
2. Lead Intake and Response Automation for small businesses
3. Research briefs and market intelligence products
4. Listing and catalog maintenance services
5. Productized software subscriptions
6. Trading or treasury automation only after separate risk gates

## Lane 1: Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service
### Offer
- Connect a client's mailbox with explicit authorization.
- Classify inbound emails into customer, vendor, invoice, support, and spam.
- Extract contact and company details from consenting, business-relevant threads.
- Normalize the data into the client's CRM or spreadsheet.
- Produce weekly summaries, stale-thread alerts, and follow-up drafts for human
  review.

### Why this lane fits
- Uses first-party client data rather than scraped third-party data.
- Creates clear operational value for SMBs with overloaded inboxes.
- Can be delivered manually at first and automated progressively.

### Delivery workflow
1. Obtain written client authorization and scope.
2. Configure a dedicated mailbox integration or exported mailbox ingestion.
3. Run RAG/search over approved content only.
4. Extract structured fields into CRM-safe schema.
5. Queue uncertain cases for human validation.
6. Deliver weekly report with metrics:
   - messages processed
   - contacts normalized
   - stale opportunities surfaced
   - estimated admin hours saved

### Pricing ideas
- Setup fee plus monthly retainer.
- Usage tier by mailbox volume.
- Optional add-on for CRM sync and response drafting.

## Lane 2: Lead Intake and Response Automation
### Offer
- Unify website forms, contact inboxes, and booking requests.
- Auto-triage incoming requests.
- Draft compliant replies and route hot leads to the owner.
- Maintain a clean opportunity board.

### Target customers
- Agencies
- Local service businesses
- B2B consultants
- Small SaaS teams

### Metrics
- First-response time
- Lead-to-meeting conversion
- Unanswered inquiry count
- Time saved per week

## Lane 3: Research Briefs and Market Intelligence
### Offer
- Produce niche market scans, competitor snapshots, or sourcing briefs.
- Sell as one-off deliverables or subscription reports.

### Constraints
- Use public, licensed, or client-provided data only.
- Cite sources and preserve evidence chains.

## Lane 4: Listing and Catalog Maintenance
### Offer
- Maintain marketplace listings, product catalogs, or directory entries.
- Improve titles, descriptions, categorization, and update cadence.

### Constraints
- Only operate on client-owned assets or assets where the client has management
  rights.

## Lane 5: Productized Software
### Offer examples
- A dashboard for inbox triage metrics
- A lightweight CRM sync service
- A proposal follow-up copilot

### Entry condition
- Do not build until one or more service lanes show repeated demand and stable
  workflows worth productizing.

## Lane 6: Trading or Treasury Automation
This lane is **deferred**. It is higher risk and should not be used as an early
cash-generation strategy.

### Preconditions
- Separate risk policy and paper-trading period
- Verified accounting and reserve tracking
- Maximum drawdown thresholds
- Human approval for live deployment

## Operating Cadence
### Daily
- Review pipeline health
- Process delivery queues
- Capture blockers in the journal

### Weekly
- Score active opportunities
- Compare gross margin, automation coverage, and delivery risk
- Retire weak lanes and reinforce strong ones

### Monthly
- Update pricing
- Productize repeated workflows
- Expand templates, prompts, and instruments

## Stop/Reject Rules
Reject any plan that relies on:
- selling scraped or compiled personal email lists
- sending unsolicited bulk email
- unauthorized inbox access
- hidden affiliate placements or deception
- regulated financial activity without controls and approvals

## Recommended Next Actions
1. Pilot the Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service with synthetic or explicitly approved
   sample data.
2. Use the scoring instrument before activating any new lane.
3. Keep a mission journal with customer hypotheses, pricing experiments, and
   operational lessons.
