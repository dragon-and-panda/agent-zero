# Agentic Financial System - Revenue Plan

This plan converts the broad mission of "self-sustaining financial system" into compliant revenue lanes with explicit sequencing, success signals, and rejection criteria.

## Mission Objective

Build a portfolio of legal, ethical, automation-friendly online revenue systems that can operate with limited human intervention while preserving user consent, data privacy, and platform compliance.

## Rejected Revenue Tactics

The following are out of scope and should be rejected by design:

- Harvesting or scraping personal email addresses without consent.
- Selling, brokering, or renting personal contact lists.
- Using inbox data from accounts the operator does not own or control.
- Sending bulk outreach that violates anti-spam rules or platform terms.
- Creating deceptive offers, fake testimonials, hidden affiliate funnels, or undisclosed paid placements.

## Active Revenue Lanes

### Lane 1: Consent-Based Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service

Offer a service for a client who explicitly authorizes access to their own inbox, files, and customer records.

Core workflow:
1. Ingest client-owned exports or API data with clear authorization.
2. Use RAG to classify conversations, identify customer intents, and extract structured records.
3. Produce a clean CRM, support backlog, FAQ corpus, and follow-up tasks.
4. Deliver value through cleanup, deduplication, routing, and analytics.

Why this lane fits:
- Immediate value to businesses with messy inboxes.
- Can be productized into repeatable agent workflows.
- Uses the repo's knowledge and memory systems naturally.

### Lane 2: Autonomous Listing and Resale Operations

Use the existing Autonomous Listing Service direction to turn owned inventory or client inventory into polished listings and managed sales workflows.

Core workflow:
1. Intake seller-authorized photos and notes.
2. Generate improved listing copy and pricing support.
3. Publish to supported channels where allowed.
4. Manage replies and negotiation inside approved guardrails.

Why this lane fits:
- Strong alignment with existing docs and service scaffold.
- Natural path to measurable cash flow.
- Does not rely on third-party personal data resale.

### Lane 3: Research Products and Market Intelligence

Package recurring research into useful paid outputs such as market maps, competitor briefs, pricing monitors, and procurement summaries.

Core workflow:
1. Gather public information and licensed data.
2. Normalize insights into a reusable knowledge base.
3. Publish reports, alerts, or dashboards for subscribers or clients.
4. Sell subscriptions, retainers, or custom projects.

Why this lane fits:
- High leverage for RAG and autonomous synthesis.
- Strong compliance profile when sources are lawful and documented.
- Useful hedge if service-based lanes fluctuate.

### Lane 4: Courseware, Templates, and Operational Playbooks

Turn internal playbooks into paid digital assets:

- SOP packs
- prompt bundles
- listing templates
- client onboarding kits
- dashboard/report templates

Why this lane fits:
- Low marginal cost after creation.
- Can be produced from internal mission journals and validated patterns.
- Supports compounding knowledge reuse.

## Hold Lanes

These may be explored later but should not be primary until the active lanes show traction:

- Affiliate sites that depend on opaque SEO risk.
- Live financial trading or automated capital deployment.
- Any product requiring regulated licenses before launch.

## Scoring Model

Every candidate lane should be scored with the current instrument inputs:

- legality
- consent
- repeatability
- time_to_cash
- defensibility
- setup_effort

If legality or consent is weak, reject the lane even if other scores are high.

## Sequencing

1. Start with Lane 1 because it is serviceable, consent-based, and maps well to RAG.
2. Keep Lane 2 active as an adjacent revenue hedge using existing repo direction.
3. Build Lane 3 in parallel at lower intensity to create IP and recurring value.
4. Convert repeatable wins into Lane 4 products.

## Near-Term Deliverables

- intake checklist for client-authorized inbox/drive exports
- extraction schema for contacts, threads, companies, intents, and tasks
- mission diary for experiments and metrics
- strategy scoring instrument for new opportunities
- compliance packet for all future automation runs

## Exit Criteria For Promotion

A lane is ready for deeper automation when it demonstrates:

- clear legal basis and documented consent
- repeatable workflow with low ambiguity handoffs
- measurable customer value
- acceptable unit economics
- low platform-enforcement risk
