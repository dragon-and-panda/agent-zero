# Agentic Financial System Charter

## Mission
Build a self-sustaining portfolio of lawful, ethical, online revenue streams that Agent Zero can help research, operate, and improve with minimal human intervention.

## Non-Negotiable Constraints
- No resale, brokerage, or scraping of personal email lists or other personal data.
- No spam, deceptive outreach, account abuse, or impersonation.
- No access to inboxes or files without explicit owner authorization.
- No regulated financial activity without the required approvals, records, and controls.

## Success Definition
The system is successful when it:
1. Creates repeatable value for customers.
2. Uses first-party or explicitly authorized data only.
3. Maintains auditability through docs, logs, and versioned playbooks.
4. Improves unit economics over time without increasing compliance risk.

## Approved Phase-1 Revenue Lanes
1. **Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service**
   - Customer grants access to their own mailbox exports or APIs.
   - Agent extracts contacts, threads, follow-up obligations, and CRM records for the customer only.
   - Deliverables: cleaned CRM, response queue, summaries, and automations.
2. **Lead Research Product**
   - Build buyer lists from public business sources and first-party form submissions.
   - Focus on business contacts and company records, not scraped personal inboxes.
3. **Autonomous Listing / Brokerage Operations**
   - Publish, enrich, and maintain customer-owned listings while enforcing compliance checks.
4. **Research + Content Assets**
   - Produce reports, playbooks, templates, and premium workflow packs for sale.

## Deferred / Conditional Lanes
- Paid outbound services only with customer-owned opt-in lists and clear compliance checks.
- Trading or treasury strategies only after paper-trading, risk controls, and reserve thresholds are met.

## Operating Loop
1. Intake new ideas into `docs/strategy/incoming.md`.
2. Score them with `instruments/strategy/score.sh`.
3. Advance only GO lanes into execution.
4. Record progress and blockers in the mission journal.
5. Feed lessons into the improvements backlog.
