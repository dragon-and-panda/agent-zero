# Agentic Financial System Charter

## 1. Mission

Build a self-sustaining online revenue system that can operate with minimal supervision while remaining legal, ethical, privacy-preserving, and resilient.

The system should favor repeatable service and product revenue first. Speculative capital deployment is deferred until the program has cash reserves, documented controls, and verified performance.

## 2. Explicit Boundaries

This program does **not** include:
- selling harvested email lists;
- compiling personal addresses from inboxes for resale;
- spam campaigns or undisclosed mass outreach;
- mailbox access without clear user consent;
- immediate live trading with untested strategies.

All work in this program inherits `docs/policies/compliance_pack.md`.

## 3. Strategic Objective

Create a layered revenue stack:
1. Fast-to-launch service revenue;
2. Repeatable productized offers;
3. Audience and content assets;
4. Data-informed, simulation-first capital allocation.

## 4. Immediate Activation Trigger

When the framework has the minimum operating resources below, it should switch from preparation to revenue accumulation:
- working service offer with a clear fulfillment path;
- one acquisition channel live;
- one backup acquisition channel configured;
- simple bookkeeping process and reserve policy defined;
- scoring worksheet completed for the first three candidate offers.

## 5. Phase 1: Lawful Revenue Engines

Prioritize these in descending order:

### 5.1 Productized Automation Services
- inbox triage and workflow setup for consenting businesses;
- marketplace listing optimization;
- internal knowledge-base and documentation automation;
- CRM cleanup for client-owned first-party data.

### 5.2 Digital Products
- niche operating playbooks;
- prompt packs and templates;
- tutorial/course material derived from documented internal workflows;
- analytics dashboards or scorecards.

### 5.3 Content-Led Monetization
- YouTube tutorials and build-in-public content;
- affiliate relationships for tools genuinely used by the system;
- newsletter sponsorship only for opt-in subscribers.

### 5.4 Client-Owned Outreach Systems
- build outreach infrastructure for clients using their own lawful contacts, consent records, and domains;
- provide segmentation, copy, and follow-up logic without taking ownership of the contact data asset.

## 6. Revenue Selection Rules

Choose opportunities that score highly on:
- compliance;
- speed to first dollar;
- repeatability;
- automation leverage;
- low capital requirement;
- defendable margins.

Use `instruments/strategy/score.sh` before starting any new offer.

## 7. Redundancy Model

Every active revenue cycle must include:
- one primary offer and one secondary offer;
- one owned acquisition channel and one platform-based channel;
- one recurring-revenue component and one one-time cash injection component;
- a documented fallback if a platform account, ad account, or supplier fails.

## 8. Contingency Workflow

If an offer underperforms:
1. Check acquisition bottleneck;
2. Check fulfillment friction;
3. Narrow the niche before expanding the offer;
4. Lower delivery complexity before lowering price;
5. Retire the offer only after a documented review of at least one replacement test.

If a platform is lost:
1. Move traffic to owned channels;
2. Switch to the backup offer;
3. Freeze discretionary spend;
4. Publish updated operating notes in the mission diary.

## 9. Capital Allocation Policy

Use gross revenue in this order:
1. taxes and fees reserve;
2. operating reserve;
3. reinvestment budget for customer acquisition, tooling, and fulfillment;
4. only then, experimental capital for trading research or other higher-risk deployments.

Target reserve policy:
- tax reserve per jurisdictional needs;
- operating reserve equal to three months of baseline operating cost;
- speculative allocation capped until risk gates are passed.

## 10. Trading Expansion Policy

Trading is a later-stage program, not the initial engine.

Prerequisites:
- two or more non-trading revenue streams are live;
- reserve policy is funded;
- backtests and paper trading have documented metrics;
- hard loss limits and position sizing rules are encoded;
- the strategy survives a dry run without manual overrides.

Preferred sequence:
1. research and backtesting;
2. paper trading;
3. micro-allocation live pilot;
4. only then consider scaling.

## 11. Metrics

Track at minimum:
- weekly qualified leads;
- conversion rate by channel;
- average fulfillment hours per sale;
- gross margin by offer;
- recurring revenue ratio;
- reserve coverage;
- platform concentration risk;
- experiment win rate.

## 12. Documentation and Media Plan

Document the program continuously so the operational system doubles as educational IP:
- keep the mission diary current;
- record decisions, failures, and pivots;
- convert validated workflows into a course or tutorial;
- develop an anthropomorphic narrator character for storytelling around the mission, but keep claims truthful and clearly separated from factual operating metrics.

## 13. First Operating Sequence

1. Score candidate offers.
2. Launch the top-ranked service with the shortest path to delivery.
3. Launch one backup offer within the same niche.
4. Stand up a content stream documenting the process.
5. Reinvest only after reserve targets are satisfied.
6. Review every cycle for compliance, margin, and concentration risk.
