# Agentic Financial System Charter

This program defines a lawful and self-sustaining revenue engine for the super-agency. The goal is to generate durable online income with high automation, low supervision, and clear downside controls.

---

## 1. Mission

Build multiple online revenue streams that:

- Rely on first-party, consent-based, or licensed data
- Can be operated by autonomous workflows with auditability
- Produce cash flow before requiring meaningful capital at risk
- Reinforce one another through shared content, tooling, and audience growth

---

## 2. Non-Goals

This program does not:

- Harvest or sell personal email lists
- Run spam or deceptive outreach
- Automate fund transfers to personal accounts without review
- Treat speculative trading as an early-stage primary revenue source

These are barred by `docs/policies/compliance_pack.md`.

---

## 3. Approved Revenue Tracks

### Track A: Consent-Based Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service

Offer a service for organizations or creators who want their own inbox, newsletter replies, or support mail cleaned and structured.

- **Inputs:** Customer-authorized Gmail or workspace data, or CSV exports
- **Output:** Deduplicated contact graph, tags, next-step recommendations, and CRM-ready export
- **Tooling:** OAuth or CSV import, RAG over customer SOPs, Orange Data Mining for segmentation and cleanup
- **Monetization:** Setup fee plus monthly maintenance retainer

### Track B: Autonomous Listing and Marketplace Operations

Use the existing listing framework in this repo to provide productized resale or client listing operations.

- **Inputs:** Owned inventory or client-authorized inventory data
- **Output:** Listings, pricing, message handling, and post-sale telemetry
- **Monetization:** Per-listing fees, monthly retainers, or revenue-share where lawful and documented

### Track C: Education and Content Engine

Document the buildout and turn it into tutorials, templates, and media.

- **Outputs:** Course modules, prompt packs, checklists, YouTube videos, and newsletters
- **Brand Device:** An anthropomorphic narrator pursuing funds for a mech suit and robot body as a storytelling layer
- **Monetization:** Digital sales, sponsorships, affiliates, and audience-driven service demand

### Track D: Internal Tools and Templates

Package reusable automations that emerge from Tracks A-C.

- **Outputs:** Intake forms, SOP packs, scoring tools, dashboards, and niche workflow kits
- **Monetization:** Templates, memberships, private repos, or consulting bundles

---

## 4. Activation Logic

Run at least three tracks in parallel once the following are true:

- Compliance pack is loaded into the workflow
- One service offer has a clear landing page or proposal template
- Intake and scoring are operational
- A simple bookkeeping and payout review workflow exists

This creates redundancy: if one channel slows, content and product channels keep feeding the system.

---

## 5. Risk Controls

- No single platform should represent the majority of new revenue.
- No initiative touching personal data launches without a source registry and consent record.
- Trading, if ever pursued, is a later treasury experiment after stable service revenue exists.
- Keep a documented backlog of contingencies for platform bans, chargebacks, and pipeline outages.

---

## 6. Immediate Priority Order

1. Package the compliant service offers.
2. Build the intake, scoring, and proposal workflow.
3. Launch the first audience and content loop.
4. Expand using templates and repeatable delivery.
5. Evaluate a paper-trading research lane only after the above are stable.

---

## 7. Success Criteria

- Revenue comes from at least two independent channels.
- Every client-facing workflow has a documented lawful basis and delivery SOP.
- The content engine creates both trust and inbound demand.
- The system can pause risky ideas before money or reputation is lost.
