# Agentic Financial System Charter

This program translates the sponsor's broad "autonomous revenue" goal into a legal, ethical, and resilient operating system. It explicitly replaces prohibited tactics such as selling harvested email lists with consent-based service delivery, productized automation, and documented reinvestment rules.

---

## 1. Mission

Build a self-sustaining portfolio of online revenue streams that can be operated by Agent Zero with low supervision, while maintaining:
- legal and ethical acquisition,
- durable unit economics,
- redundancy across channels,
- auditability of decisions and payouts.

---

## 2. Hard Constraints

This program inherits all rules from `docs/policies/compliance_pack.md`.

Especially important:
- no harvesting or selling personal email lists,
- no inbox data resale,
- no spam-based lead generation,
- no unbounded speculative trading as an early-stage dependency.

---

## 3. Program Architecture

### 3.1 Core loops
1. **Opportunity intake:** New ideas land in `docs/strategy/incoming.md`.
2. **Scoring:** Each idea is scored with `instruments/strategy/score.sh`.
3. **Selection:** Opportunities are labeled `GO`, `HOLD`, or `REJECT`.
4. **Execution:** The top `GO` opportunities are productized into repeatable playbooks.
5. **Documentation:** Results, blockers, and next bets are logged in `journal.md`.
6. **Refinement:** New improvements are tracked in `improvements.md`.

### 3.2 Revenue portfolio model
The system should avoid single-point dependency by operating at least three revenue tracks:

| Track | Type | Why it exists |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Service cash flow | Done-for-you or done-with-you work | Fastest route to validated revenue |
| Productized assets | Templates, SOPs, reports, prompt packs, courses | Higher margin and scalable |
| Audience/content engine | Tutorials, video stories, case studies | Reduces acquisition cost over time |

Trading or investing stays outside the initial portfolio until the operating business produces stable cash flow and the risk framework is documented.

## 4. Approved Phase 1 Revenue Plays

### 4.1 Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service
Use RAG over a consenting client's own email corpus to:
- identify active contacts,
- deduplicate records,
- tag relationships,
- produce a private CRM export,
- generate suggested follow-up tasks.

Revenue model:
- setup fee,
- monthly maintenance,
- optional analytics upsell.

### 4.2 Autonomous Listing Concierge
Use the existing listing-service blueprint to turn photos, notes, and pricing comps into high-quality marketplace listings for clients or internal resale inventory.

Revenue model:
- per listing fee,
- success fee,
- add-ons for negotiation handling or relisting.

### 4.3 Public-Web Research Briefs
Produce structured research packs from public, licensed, or client-provided data:
- competitor maps,
- prospect/account briefs,
- supplier scouting,
- category trend summaries.

Revenue model:
- fixed-fee brief,
- retainer,
- recurring market watch product.

### 4.4 Course + Character Content Funnel
Document processes honestly and turn them into:
- short tutorials,
- a lightweight course,
- a story-driven content series built around the mech-suit/robot-body narrative.

Revenue model:
- ad revenue,
- sponsorships,
- course sales,
- consulting inbound generated by content.

## 5. Data Strategy

### 5.1 Gmail / email usage
The email-data component must be reframed from "list acquisition" to "private relationship intelligence" for the data owner.

Allowed outputs:
- contact graph,
- relationship summaries,
- internal CRM enrichment,
- response drafting context,
- pipeline snapshots.

Blocked outputs:
- brokerable lists,
- cold outreach lists built from private correspondence,
- third-party marketing datasets derived from inbox metadata.

### 5.2 Orange workflow
Orange may be used for:
- clustering contacts by relationship strength,
- segmenting opted-in customers,
- identifying repeat conversations or renewal opportunities,
- visualizing performance of consent-based funnels.

## 6. Launch Sequence

### Stage A: Foundation
- Finalize policy pack and scoring rubric.
- Populate intake queue with at least five candidate ventures.
- Select the top two `GO` opportunities.

### Stage B: Revenue activation
- Launch one fast service and one slower compounding asset.
- Build minimum viable delivery pipeline, offer page, and intake checklist.
- Require a documented fallback if a platform or acquisition source fails.

### Stage C: Scaling
- Add templates, case studies, and automation modules from successful delivery work.
- Move repeatable outputs into productized offers.
- Reinvest a capped portion of profits into tooling, traffic, or experiments.

### Stage D: Optional capital deployment
- Only after stable operating revenue.
- Start with simulated trading or very small, capped live tests.
- Stop immediately if drawdown or variance exceeds predefined bounds.

---

## 7. Redundancy and Contingency Design

### 7.1 Acquisition redundancy
- Primary: referral/outbound based on existing relationships or public business data.
- Secondary: content funnel.
- Tertiary: marketplace/platform discovery.

### 7.2 Delivery redundancy
- Each service should have:
  - one automated path,
  - one manual fallback,
  - one alternative tool/vendor.

### 7.3 Revenue protection
- Avoid customer concentration where one client represents most income.
- Keep at least two independent offers active.
- Document kill-switches for policy or platform failures.

---

## 8. Definition of "Sufficient Resources"

The system may begin active revenue accumulation when all of the following are true:
- a compliant offer is scored `GO`,
- intake and delivery steps are documented,
- one fallback channel exists for acquisition or fulfillment,
- bookkeeping and payout handling are defined,
- the workflow can run without prohibited data use.

At that moment, the next action is not "collect more data"; it is:
1. pick the highest-confidence `GO` offer,
2. publish the intake + offer materials,
3. start outreach only to opted-in, public, or relationship-based targets,
4. log results and iterate weekly.
