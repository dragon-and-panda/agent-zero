# Agentic Financial System - Charter

This program defines a lawful, consent-based path for building a self-sustaining online revenue engine with low human supervision. The objective is not "make money by any means"; the objective is to compound revenue through services and media that remain ethical, documented, and automatable.

## 1. Mission

Build a multi-lane autonomous business system that:

- reaches first revenue through online services with low startup cost,
- converts repeatable work into tools, templates, and agents,
- reinvests profits into safer automation and distribution,
- and only expands into higher-risk activities after operational cash flow exists.

## 2. Explicitly Out of Scope

The following are rejected:

- harvesting or brokering personal email lists,
- selling inbox-derived contact data,
- unconsented Gmail extraction for marketing,
- spam-based acquisition,
- and any workflow that depends on violating privacy law or platform terms.

If email is used at all, it must be for **first-party retrieval, CRM cleanup, support search, or relationship intelligence** on behalf of the account owner.

## 3. Phase 1 Revenue Lanes

### Lane A - Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service
Use RAG over a consenting client's own email archive to build:

- a private searchable knowledge base,
- a deduplicated contact map,
- relationship notes,
- follow-up tasks,
- and an export into a CRM or spreadsheet.

Why this goes first:

- fast to explain,
- low capital requirement,
- tangible deliverable,
- strong fit for automation and repeatable SOPs.

### Lane B - Autonomous Listing Concierge
Use the listing-service blueprint already in this repo to help sellers convert rough notes and photos into polished listings and buyer-response packages.

### Lane C - Public-Web Research Briefs
Offer repeatable research products using public and client-provided data:

- supplier maps,
- market scans,
- competitor snapshots,
- and operating playbooks.

### Lane D - Course + Character Content Funnel
Once the first three lanes produce real proof, convert the operating system into:

- tutorials,
- a course,
- a YouTube storyline,
- and downloadable playbooks.

Narrative concept: an anthropomorphic guide documents the grind of earning enough to fund a mech suit and, later, a robot body. The character is brand packaging, not a reason to weaken compliance.

## 4. Redundancy and Loss Mitigation

The system should never depend on a single revenue source. Default redundancy:

1. one short-cycle service lane for immediate cash conversion,
2. one asset-backed lane using existing repo capabilities,
3. one research lane that depends only on public information,
4. one media lane that compounds trust and demand over time.

Contingency logic:

- If client acquisition slows for Lane A, shift spare capacity into Lanes B and C.
- If platform risk rises for Lane B, increase emphasis on Lanes A and C.
- If service delivery saturates, productize outputs into Lane D.
- If all live offers soften, collect case studies, improve the scorecard, and narrow ICP before adding more complexity.

## 5. Capital Allocation Rules

Priority order for early cash:

1. infrastructure and automation,
2. bookkeeping and operational resilience,
3. customer acquisition experiments with clear attribution,
4. only then controlled experimentation in trading.

Forex or other active trading must remain a later-stage experiment. Required before any live trading:

- written rules,
- paper-trade history,
- max daily and weekly loss caps,
- no borrowed capital,
- and no use of essential operating cash.

## 6. Operating Loop

1. Add or update opportunities in `docs/strategy/incoming.md`.
2. Score them with `instruments/strategy/score.sh`.
3. Reject anything that fails legality, consent, or data-sensitivity gates.
4. Launch one narrowly scoped pilot per approved lane.
5. Log delivery results and blockers in the mission journal.
6. Promote repeated steps into instruments, prompts, or knowledge packs.

## 7. Initial KPIs

- time to first qualified offer,
- time to first dollar collected,
- gross margin by lane,
- repeatability of delivery,
- compliance incidents,
- and percentage of work converted into reusable assets.

## 8. Funds Handling

This repo can support operations, scoring, and documentation, but it should not automatically move money. Payout routing, including any Cash App destination, must only happen after lawful earnings exist and the operator has separately verified account ownership, recordkeeping, and tax handling.
