# Agentic Financial System — Charter

This program defines an ethical, legal, and low-touch path for using Agent Zero to build a self-sustaining online income system. The goal is not "growth at any cost"; the goal is durable revenue from consent-based services, products, and content that can be operated with increasing autonomy over time.

---

## 1. Mission

Build a redundant portfolio of online revenue engines that:
- can be operated or supervised by agents,
- do not depend on privacy-invasive data collection,
- avoid unlawful, deceptive, or spam-oriented tactics,
- reinvest profits into safer, higher-leverage digital assets before any speculative capital deployment.

---

## 2. Hard Guardrails

The following are out of scope for this program and must be treated as **REJECT** opportunities:

- Harvesting email addresses or contact lists from inboxes for resale.
- Selling, brokering, renting, or trading personal email lists.
- Using Gmail, Google Workspace, or other private mailbox data without explicit account-owner authorization and a legitimate operational purpose.
- Sending spam, cold outreach at abusive volume, or bypassing platform anti-abuse controls.
- Scraping private or access-controlled datasets in violation of platform terms or law.
- Running autonomous live trading with leverage as an initial monetization step.
- Offering regulated financial advice or managing third-party funds without the required licensing and controls.

Allowed mailbox usage is limited to **operator-owned or client-authorized workflow automation**, such as inbox triage, CRM hygiene, relationship mapping, and response drafting, with minimum necessary access and auditable consent.

---

## 3. Operating Principles

1. **Consent first:** Only use data that is owned by the operator, explicitly provided by a client, or sourced from public/opt-in channels.
2. **Fast time to cash:** Prioritize offers that can be sold quickly without paid acquisition.
3. **Automation bias:** Favor workflows that can be standardized into prompts, instruments, or API-backed services.
4. **Portfolio redundancy:** Maintain multiple revenue streams so any single platform, client, or tactic can fail without stopping the system.
5. **Capital protection:** Protect runway before pursuing higher-risk expansion.
6. **Document everything:** Turn each repeatable workflow into SOPs, prompts, datasets, and media assets that compound.

---

## 4. Revenue Portfolio

### 4.1 Phase 1 — Immediate Revenue Engines

These are the default "GO" tracks for early revenue:

| Track | Description | Why it fits |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service | With explicit client consent, extract contacts, threads, and relationship metadata from a business inbox and organize them into CRM-ready records, follow-up queues, and summaries. | Fast to sell, high operational value, legal when consented, strongly automatable. |
| Public-Web Research Briefs | Produce niche prospect lists, market maps, vendor comparisons, and research briefs from public sources only. | No private-data dependency, reusable templates, easy productization. |
| Autonomous Listing Concierge | Turn seller-provided assets into polished listings, marketplace syndication, and buyer-response support. | Already aligned with repo docs and service concepts. |
| Course + Character Content Funnel | Publish educational content showing how the system works, narrated by a branded anthropomorphic character. | Builds audience, trust, inbound leads, and future product demand. |

### 4.2 Phase 2 — Productization

Once at least one service has repeatable delivery:

- package deliverables into templates, checklists, and prompt kits,
- sell fixed-scope audits or setup packages,
- offer subscriptions for monitoring, reporting, or monthly optimization,
- convert the best workflows into MCP-backed or API-backed services.

### 4.3 Phase 3 — Capital Deployment

Only after revenue is stable and operating cash is protected:

1. Maintain a reserve buffer for operating expenses and failed experiments.
2. Test trading ideas only in **paper trading** or tightly capped simulation.
3. If any market strategy advances beyond research, start with low-risk, rules-based, fully logged experimentation.
4. Treat leveraged Forex as **HOLD** until the following exist:
   - written rules,
   - drawdown limits,
   - paper-trading evidence,
   - compliance review,
   - explicit human approval for live capital.

## 5. Immediate Plan to Begin Accumulating Revenue

### 5.1 Order of Operations

1. **Fill the opportunity queue** in `docs/strategy/incoming.md`.
2. **Score each opportunity** using `instruments/strategy/score.sh`.

   - Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service

| Step | Action | Output |

## 6. Data Strategy

### 6.1 Permitted Data Sources
- Public websites and public business directories.
- Operator-created knowledge bases and notes.
- Client-owned mailbox or CRM data with explicit authorization.
- Seller-provided product/listing data.
- First-party analytics from owned content channels and landing pages.

### 6.2 Restricted Data Sources
- Personal inboxes without a valid business purpose and explicit consent.
- Private email threads used to build sellable mailing lists.
- Purchased or scraped personal data of uncertain provenance.
- Data exports that violate retention, confidentiality, or platform terms.

### 6.3 RAG Use

RAG is appropriate for:
- retrieving SOPs, playbooks, offer templates, and prior delivery artifacts,
- summarizing authorized inboxes for CRM hygiene or relationship follow-up,
- turning course notes and journals into scripts, outlines, and FAQ content.

RAG is not appropriate for:
- mining private correspondence to create spam lists,
- extracting contact data for resale,
- using private communications outside the scope of the original consent.

---

## 7. Redundancy and Loss Mitigation

The system should always have:

- **At least three active opportunity lanes:** one service, one product/content lane, one research lane.
- **Channel redundancy:** avoid dependence on one marketplace or one acquisition channel.
- **Delivery redundancy:** keep SOPs, templates, and prompts versioned in the repo.
- **Financial controls:** ring-fence operating cash from experimental spend.
- **Decision checkpoints:** require GO scores before adding new initiatives.

Contingencies:

- If a service stalls, re-score adjacent niches using the same fulfillment backbone.
- If a platform account is limited, pivot to owned channels and direct fulfillment.
- If content underperforms, repurpose journal entries into short-form, long-form, and email-safe educational assets.
- If trading research underperforms, keep it in paper mode and redeploy effort to services with proven cash flow.

---

## 8. Content System and Character

The documentation stream doubles as a content engine:

- **Journal** captures what was attempted, what worked, and what failed.
- **Improvements backlog** surfaces the next experiments.
- **Course outline** can be built from recurring SOPs and case studies.
- **Anthropomorphic narrator** should function as a brand device, not a deception layer.

Suggested character brief:
- role: determined builder-engineer,
- tone: curious, resilient, technically sharp,
- story arc: assembling a sustainable digital workshop that gradually funds increasingly ambitious hardware goals,
- output formats: narrated devlogs, explainer shorts, tutorials, retrospectives.

---

## 9. Success Metrics

Track at least:
- opportunities scored per week,
- GO / HOLD / REJECT ratio,
- time from idea to first invoice,
- gross revenue by lane,
- percentage of fulfillment steps automated,
- reusable assets created per delivery,
- content pieces produced from operating work,
- capital reserve coverage before any speculative deployment.
