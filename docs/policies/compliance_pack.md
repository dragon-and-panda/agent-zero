# Monetization Compliance Pack

This pack defines the minimum compliance rules for any revenue-seeking workflow operated through the repo.

---

## 1. Core Policy

Revenue generation must be:
- legal in the operator's jurisdiction,
- honest about source data and outcomes,
- based on consent or public information,
- reversible or containable when risk is uncertain,
- documented well enough for audit and review.

If a tactic depends on deception, privacy invasion, spam, credential misuse, or unclear data provenance, it must be treated as non-compliant.

---

## 2. Contact Data and Inbox Rules

### Allowed
- Organizing contacts from a client-owned inbox when the client has explicitly authorized the work.
- Deduplicating, tagging, and summarizing business contacts for CRM hygiene.
- Extracting public business contact information from websites or directories where collection and use are permitted.
- Drafting follow-ups for existing relationships under the account owner's normal business process.

### Not allowed
- Reselling email addresses collected from sent, received, or CC fields.
- Building mass-marketing lists from private inboxes.
- Mining personal correspondence for unrelated commercial use.
- Sending unsolicited bulk outreach that violates law, platform rules, or recipient expectations.
- Circumventing platform limits, CAPTCHAs, or anti-abuse systems to collect contacts at scale.

Required controls:
- documented authorization,
- minimum necessary data access,
- retention limits,
- deletion path on request,
- audit trail of what was extracted and why.

---

## 3. Public-Web Research Rules

Public-web research is allowed when:
- the source is publicly accessible without bypassing access controls,
- collection respects site terms and robots guidance where applicable,
- outputs are sold as analysis, organization, or enrichment work,
- sensitive personal data is not repackaged into harmful targeting products.

Prefer:
- business profiles,
- product catalogs,
- public case studies,
- public pricing pages,
- public team and partnership information.

Avoid:
- doxxing,
- personal targeting,
- data broker behavior,
- scraping sites that clearly prohibit the intended use.

---

## 4. Content and Marketing Rules

- Educational content must accurately describe what the system does.
- Do not fabricate case studies, customer results, or identity.
- Brand characters are allowed as storytelling devices, but they must not impersonate real people or conceal material facts.
- Tutorials should teach consent-based automation, public-data research, and documented experimentation.

---

## 5. Financial Risk Rules

- No live leveraged trading as a first monetization step.
- No management of third-party capital.
- No claims of guaranteed returns.
- All market strategies must begin with paper trading and written risk controls.
- Capital experiments must use predefined limits, logging, and human review.

Minimum preconditions before any live strategy test:
- documented hypothesis,
- entry/exit rules,
- drawdown limit,
- stop condition,
- result log,
- reserve capital untouched by experimentation.

---

## 6. Opportunity Gating

Every new opportunity must be labeled:
- **GO**: legal, ethical, quick to validate, and operationally feasible.
- **HOLD**: potentially valuable but blocked on controls, capital, or proof.
- **REJECT**: privacy-invasive, deceptive, unlawful, or structurally fragile.

Automatic REJECT triggers:
- resale of private contact data,
- spam-as-a-business-model,
- unauthorized account access,
- evasion of platform safeguards,
- monetization based on stolen, leaked, or ambiguous data.

---

## 7. Review Questions

Before execution, answer:
1. Who owns the data?
2. What consent or legal basis exists?
3. What is being sold: insight, service, software, or raw personal data?
4. Could the workflow harm recipients, clients, or bystanders?
5. Is there an auditable record of scope and output?
6. If this workflow became public, would it still be defensible?

If any answer is unclear, the opportunity stays on HOLD or moves to REJECT.
