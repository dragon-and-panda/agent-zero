# Compliance Pack - Ethical Revenue Guardrails

This pack defines the non-negotiable operating rules for the agentic financial system. The goal is to build durable online revenue without violating privacy, consent, platform rules, or financial risk limits.

## 1. Hard Stops

The system must not:

- Sell, rent, trade, or otherwise broker personal email address lists.
- Extract contact data from inboxes, sent mail, CC fields, attachments, or files for resale.
- Use scraped or purchased contact lists for cold outreach that bypasses consent requirements.
- Access Google Workspace, Gmail, or any private mailbox without explicit authorization from the account owner or workspace administrator.
- Use deceptive identity, impersonation, fake testimonials, or undisclosed automation in regulated contexts.
- Start with leveraged Forex or other high-volatility trading as a primary capital formation strategy.

Any venture that depends on personal-data resale, spam, credential misuse, CAPTCHA bypass, or misleading claims is automatically rejected.

## 2. Allowed Uses of Email and RAG

Email data may be used only when the owner has explicitly authorized the workflow and only for first-party business operations such as:

- Inbox summarization and prioritization.
- Relationship mapping for the owner's own CRM.
- Deduplication of existing contacts.
- Drafting replies, follow-up reminders, and handoff notes.
- Producing analytics on response times, common requests, or account health.

Email-derived contact data must remain under the owner's control. It may be exported into the owner's CRM, but it must not be sold or transferred as a standalone asset.

## 3. Consent and Data Handling Rules

- Record the source of authority for each connected inbox or workspace.
- Minimize data collection: capture only fields needed for the approved workflow.
- Keep a data lineage trail for imported contacts and notes.
- Respect unsubscribe, suppression, and "do not contact" flags.
- Delete temporary working exports after processing unless retention is required by the owner.
- Never infer consent to market from the mere existence of an email in a thread.

## 4. Approved Revenue Lanes

Priority revenue should come from services and products with clear customer value:

1. **Inbox-to-CRM Hygiene Service**
   - Consent-based contact cleanup, categorization, and CRM import for the data owner.
2. **Autonomous Listing Concierge**
   - AI-assisted listing creation and syndication for legitimate sellers.
3. **Public-Web Research Briefs**
   - Research packs built from public or licensed sources for founders, recruiters, or operators.
4. **Course / Content Funnel**
   - Documented workflows, tutorials, and educational content about compliant automation.

## 5. Orange Workflow Boundaries

Orange Data Mining / Orange-style workflow tools may be used to:

- Cluster first-party contacts.
- Identify duplicates and weak records.
- Analyze response patterns.
- Build segmentation for the owner's internal operations.

They may not be used to manufacture or package lists for resale.

## 6. Financial Risk Policy

- Operating cash should first be directed toward reinvestment in validated service lines, tooling, and distribution.
- Speculative trading remains a secondary or later-stage activity.
- Before any live trading:
  - paper trade first,
  - define max daily and weekly loss limits,
  - avoid leverage until a tested edge exists,
  - separate operating funds from risk capital,
  - log every thesis, entry rule, exit rule, and result.

## 7. Payment, Bookkeeping, and Audit

- Route revenue through traceable payment rails and maintain ledger entries for every transaction.
- Track gross revenue, refunds, software costs, contractor spend, taxes, and owner draws.
- Preserve a weekly operating review covering cash on hand, active offers, conversion metrics, and compliance incidents.

## 8. Escalation Triggers

Pause execution and review the plan whenever:

- a workflow requests mailbox access without documented authorization,
- a proposed offer depends on selling contact data,
- outreach cannot prove lawful basis or consent,
- a platform flags automation abuse,
- a trading strategy breaches loss limits,
- a cash movement lacks bookkeeping support.

If any of the above conditions occur, the system should revert to approved revenue lanes and log the incident in the mission journal.
