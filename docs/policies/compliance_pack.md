# Compliance Pack for Autonomous Monetization

This pack defines the hard boundaries for any revenue-seeking workflow built on top of Agent Zero. It is designed to keep the system profitable, durable, and compliant by rejecting short-term tactics that depend on privacy abuse, deception, spam, or unlicensed financial activity.

---

## 1. Non-Negotiable Rules

### 1.1 Prohibited data practices
- Do not extract, compile, sell, rent, trade, or transfer personal email addresses from inboxes, sent mail, CC fields, contact exports, or third-party files unless every data subject has explicitly consented to that exact use.
- Do not use Gmail, IMAP, Google Workspace, or any other inbox as a source for list brokerage, cold-spam harvesting, or shadow CRM building.
- Do not bypass platform permissions, scrape protected content, or retain personal data beyond the stated business purpose.

### 1.2 Prohibited monetization tactics
- No sale of personal email lists.
- No spam campaigns to non-opted-in recipients.
- No impersonation, fake testimonials, fabricated case studies, or undisclosed AI personas.
- No trading schemes that imply guaranteed returns or use borrowed/third-party funds without authorization.
- No regulated activity that requires licensing, registration, or disclosures unless those requirements are already satisfied.

### 1.3 Mandatory operating principles
- Consent first.
- Clear value exchange.
- Minimum necessary data.
- Human-auditable logs.
- Written kill-switch conditions for every automated workflow.

---

## 2. Approved Data Handling Patterns

### 2.1 Inbox analysis: allowed scope
Inbox analysis is only permitted for the account owner's own productivity, CRM hygiene, relationship mapping, and customer-support workflows.

Approved examples:
- Deduplicate contacts from the owner's own sent/received history.
- Tag conversations by category, urgency, or buyer/supplier relationship.
- Build a private CRM for follow-up on existing consent-based relationships.
- Use RAG over the owner's own email corpus to answer internal questions or draft responses.

Not approved:
- Exporting inbox-derived addresses for resale.
- Using historical recipients as implied marketing consent.
- Combining scraped public data with private inbox data to enlarge a cold-outreach list.

### 2.2 Orange Data Mining / analytics usage
Orange or similar tooling may be used to:
- cluster opt-in leads,
- score customer segments,
- identify churn risks,
- detect duplicate contacts,
- organize first-party datasets.

It may not be used to launder or operationalize non-consensual contact harvesting.

---

## 3. Approved Revenue Tracks

Priority should go to services and products that monetize capability instead of personal data.

### 3.1 Service businesses
- Inbox-to-CRM hygiene for consenting clients.
- Autonomous listing concierge for local resale or consignment.
- Public-web research briefs for businesses, creators, or agencies.
- Content repurposing and documentation services.

### 3.2 Productized workflows
- SOP packs, templates, and mini-courses built from documented internal processes.
- Prompt packs, scoring rubrics, and lightweight agent operations kits.
- Analytics dashboards for clients' own opted-in lead funnels.

### 3.3 Audience building
- Newsletter with explicit subscription.
- YouTube/channel content built from transparent tutorials and case studies.
- Lead magnets that collect consent before any follow-up.

---

## 4. Trading and Capital Deployment Guardrails

If capital is later deployed into trading or investing:
- Treat it as a separate risk bucket, not the primary revenue engine.
- Use only funds already earned and explicitly allocated for risk capital.
- Start with paper trading or sandbox testing before live deployment.
- Document risk limits, max drawdown, position sizing, and stop conditions.
- Do not present outputs as financial advice or guaranteed-return systems.

Hold status by default applies to leveraged Forex or any strategy that cannot be explained, tested, and capped with hard risk controls.

---

## 5. Workflow Gate Before Launch

No venture may move into execution until it passes all five checks:

1. **Consent check:** Are all data sources first-party, public, or explicitly licensed?
2. **Terms check:** Does the workflow comply with the source platform's terms?
3. **Disclosure check:** Would a reasonable customer understand what is automated and what is human?
4. **Risk check:** Is downside bounded with clear rollback or shutdown steps?
5. **Accounting check:** Can revenue, costs, taxes, and refunds be tracked cleanly?

Any "no" answer moves the idea to `REJECT` or `HOLD`.

---

## 6. Default Contingencies

- If a workflow depends on a single platform, add a second acquisition or delivery channel before scaling.
- If personal data is involved, store only what is required and define deletion windows.
- If a workflow touches messaging, require opt-in records and unsubscribe handling.
- If payout flow is unclear, do not launch until bookkeeping and platform-compliant payout rails are defined.

This pack is the canonical policy reference for the Agentic Financial System program.
