# Compliance Pack for Autonomous Revenue Programs

This pack defines the non-negotiable rules for any monetization workflow run from this repository. It is designed to keep the system focused on ethical, legal, consent-based revenue.

---

## 1. Hard Prohibitions

The system must reject, halt, or escalate any workflow that attempts to do any of the following:

- Harvest, scrape, broker, rent, or sell personal email addresses or contact lists.
- Extract contacts from inboxes, sent mail, CC lines, or attachments for resale or unsolicited bulk outreach.
- Access a mailbox, CRM, or cloud account without the account owner's explicit authorization.
- Use RAG, search, or automation to bypass privacy expectations, terms of service, or anti-spam rules.
- Send deceptive, undisclosed, or non-consensual marketing messages.
- Circumvent platform safeguards such as rate limits, identity checks, CAPTCHAs, or anti-abuse controls.
- Handle regulated financial activity as a revenue engine without the required licenses, disclosures, controls, and jurisdiction review.
- Route funds to unverified accounts or payment rails that do not match the legal owner of the business activity.

Any idea that depends on third-party personal data acquisition without consent is a **REJECT**.

---

## 2. Allowed Data Use

Data use is only allowed when all of the following are true:

1. The data belongs to the user or their client, or is clearly public and permitted for the intended use.
2. The account owner has granted informed permission for the workflow.
3. The processing benefits the owner of the data, not an unrelated third party.
4. The workflow records the data source, intended use, retention rule, and opt-out or deletion path.

Examples of allowed inbox-related uses:

- Building a private, first-party CRM for the mailbox owner.
- Deduplicating known contacts for relationship management.
- Summarizing communications to improve customer support or sales operations.
- Drafting follow-up messages only where a legitimate business relationship or explicit consent exists.

Examples of disallowed inbox-related uses:

- Converting inbox traffic into a list to sell.
- Mining CC fields to create cold-outreach databases.
- Combining mailbox contacts with scraped records to make marketing lists.

---

## 3. Approved Revenue Patterns

These patterns are aligned with the system's goals and this compliance pack:

- **Consent-based inbox-to-CRM cleanup:** organize a client's own communications, tag warm relationships, and improve follow-up workflows.
- **Autonomous listing concierge:** create and manage lawful marketplace listings for items the seller owns or is authorized to represent.
- **AI operations audits:** analyze a client's repetitive workflows and propose automations.
- **Documentation products:** turn proven workflows into tutorials, playbooks, templates, or course material.
- **Content systems:** produce branded educational content that attracts opted-in leads.

---

## 4. Financial Safety Rules

- Service revenue should come before speculative trading.
- Any later trading research must begin with simulation or paper trading only.
- No live trading should begin until the program has documented:
  - positive service cash flow,
  - an operating reserve,
  - explicit risk caps,
  - jurisdiction and platform compliance,
  - a written stop-loss and halt policy.

High-volatility trading is not treated as a primary monetization track in this repo.

---

## 5. Operational Checks Before Launch

Before any new monetization workflow is marked **GO**, the system should verify:

- Consent path is documented.
- Offer is legal in the target jurisdiction.
- Delivery steps are repeatable and auditable.
- Refund and dispute handling exists.
- Payout destination is verified and tax-trackable.
- There is a fallback path if a platform, provider, or traffic source fails.

---

## 6. Default Enforcement Outcome

When a proposed idea conflicts with this pack:

- classify it as **REJECT**,
- explain the blocked behavior,
- suggest a consent-based alternative that preserves the business goal.

For the financial system program, the default replacement for email-list resale is:

> build a first-party relationship management service for consenting owners of the inbox data, then monetize through setup fees, retainers, and adjacent automation services.
