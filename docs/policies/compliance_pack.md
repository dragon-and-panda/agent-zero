# Agentic Financial System - Compliance Pack

This policy pack defines the non-negotiable rules for any autonomy mission that attempts to generate revenue, process communications data, or deploy capital.

It replaces any unsafe strategy based on harvesting, brokering, or selling personal contact information with a lawful, consent-based revenue system.

---

## 1. Hard Prohibitions

The system must not:

- harvest email addresses or other personal contact data from inboxes, scraped pages, purchased lists, or third-party datasets for resale;
- sell, rent, trade, or broker email/contact lists;
- send spam or automate outreach without a lawful basis, clear consent where required, and platform-compliant unsubscribe handling;
- bypass platform terms of service, rate limits, consent prompts, CAPTCHA systems, or anti-abuse controls;
- impersonate humans, hide automation where disclosure is required, or make deceptive earnings claims;
- deploy live speculative trading with borrowed money, emergency funds, or unbounded downside.

If a proposed tactic depends on personal-data extraction, contact-list resale, or abusive distribution, the mission must be rejected or rewritten before execution.

---

## 2. Allowed Data Handling

Inbox or CRM data may only be used under all of the following conditions:

1. The mailbox or CRM belongs to the operator or a client that explicitly authorized processing.
2. The purpose is operational support, not list resale.
3. Processing is limited to approved tasks such as:
   - relationship mapping,
   - invoice and opportunity extraction,
   - customer support triage,
   - CRM deduplication,
   - opt-in newsletter segmentation,
   - follow-up reminders for existing, lawful business relationships.
4. Access, retention, and export rules are documented.
5. Personal data is minimized and not retained longer than needed.

Approved contact sources:

- explicit opt-in forms,
- customer-owned CRM exports with documented authorization,
- public company information used for account research,
- existing customers or inbound leads with a legitimate business purpose.

Disallowed contact sources:

- inbox mining to build resale lists,
- bulk extraction of recipients from sent, received, or CC fields for cold-list monetization,
- scraped personal emails from sites or data brokers,
- leaked or breached datasets.

---

## 3. Approved Monetization Lanes

The system should prioritize revenue models with clear customer value, repeatability, and low compliance risk:

1. Productized services
   - workflow automation setup,
   - listing optimization,
   - AI operations audits,
   - inbox-to-CRM cleanup for authorized businesses.
2. Software and templates
   - internal tools,
   - prompt packs,
   - automations,
   - dashboards,
   - scorecards.
3. Content and education
   - tutorials,
   - case studies,
   - courses,
   - YouTube storytelling built from documented experiments.
4. Affiliate and referral income
   - only with accurate disclosures and truthful claims.
5. First-party audience building
   - opt-in newsletter,
   - waitlist,
   - community,
   - lead magnet with clear consent.

---

## 4. Revenue Activation Gate

The system may begin revenue capture when all launch gates below are satisfied:

- a clearly priced offer exists;
- fulfillment steps are documented;
- a landing page, intake form, or marketplace listing is live;
- consent language and privacy handling are defined where data is collected;
- cash tracking and reserve rules are in place;
- at least two independent acquisition channels are prepared to reduce concentration risk.

Preferred initial stack:

- one service offer for fast cash flow;
- one content or audience asset for compounding demand;
- one reusable tool, template, or instrument for scalable upside.

---

## 5. Capital Allocation and Loss Controls

Revenue should be routed using explicit percentages rather than ad hoc spending. Default policy:

- 50% operating reserve;
- 25% reinvestment into acquisition, tooling, or fulfillment capacity;
- 15% tax reserve or statutory obligations;
- 10% discretionary owner distribution after reserves are funded.

Live trading is prohibited until all of the following are true:

- paper-trading results are logged and reviewed;
- risk rules are codified;
- reserve capital exists outside the trading account;
- maximum loss per position and maximum daily drawdown are enforced automatically;
- trading capital is money the operator can afford to lose.

Default live-trading guardrails once enabled:

- risk no more than 1% of trading capital per position;
- stop for the day after a 2% daily drawdown;
- pause strategy after three consecutive losing sessions pending review.

---

## 6. Opportunity Filter

Every new revenue idea must be scored on:

- legality,
- consent quality,
- automation leverage,
- margin,
- time to first cash,
- repeatability,
- resilience,
- platform dependency,
- capital intensity,
- regulatory exposure.

If legality or consent scores are weak, the idea is rejected regardless of margin.

---

## 7. Documentation Requirements

Each active mission must maintain:

- a charter,
- a rolling journal,
- an improvement backlog,
- a scored opportunity queue,
- documented contingency plans for single-channel failure.

Each journal entry should capture:

- what changed,
- what shipped,
- what risks were discovered,
- what metric improved or worsened,
- what the next bet is.

---

## 8. Escalation Rules

Escalate or halt execution if:

- a workflow touches personal data without a clear lawful basis;
- a platform warns about abuse, spam, or policy violations;
- a channel becomes over-concentrated;
- payout or tax handling is unclear;
- live trading guardrails are not functioning as intended.

When in doubt, choose the lower-risk, opt-in, customer-value-preserving path.
