# Compliance Pack for Autonomous Revenue Work

This pack defines the hard boundaries for any automated monetization workflow built in this repository.

## Non-negotiable rules
- Do not harvest, broker, scrape, enrich, or sell personal email addresses, inbox exports, contact lists, or other personal data without lawful consent.
- Do not build workflows for spam, account takeover, impersonation, deception, or circumvention of platform safeguards.
- Do not publish or sell financial promotions that imply guaranteed returns, managed-account promises, trading signals, or other regulated investment advice.
- Do not move funds to personal payment handles or third-party accounts without a human-approved treasury process, bookkeeping trail, and verified ownership.

## Allowed revenue lanes
- First-party inbox-to-CRM cleanup for the account owner, with consent and export controls.
- Marketplace listing automation for items the seller owns or is authorized to sell.
- Public-web research briefs, lead intelligence based on public business data, and opt-in demand generation.
- Content products, tutorials, templates, or software services that do not require unlawful data acquisition.

## Contact-data handling
- Gmail, inbox, and CRM data may only be used for the account owner's internal organization, deduplication, reply drafting, and first-party analytics.
- Contact exports must remain private to the data owner unless the owner initiates a lawful transfer for their own business operations.
- Any workflow touching personal communications must document purpose, retention, and deletion rules.

## Trading and treasury controls
- New capital should be deployed first into reserves and core operations, not straight into speculative trading.
- Trading research must start with paper trading, simulation, and audited risk metrics.
- Live leveraged trading remains out of scope until a later review confirms legal fit, operating reserves, and objective risk controls.

## Required operating checks
- Every offer must have a clear owner, lawful acquisition path, delivery method, and refund/remediation path.
- Every public-facing workflow must include truthful descriptions and platform-policy checks.
- High-risk domains require a manual review checkpoint before publication or payment movement.
