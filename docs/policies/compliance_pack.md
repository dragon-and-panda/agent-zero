# Compliance Pack for Autonomous Revenue Programs

This policy pack is the default guardrail set for any monetization workflow built in this repository.

---

## 1. Non-Negotiable Rules
- Only pursue revenue streams that are legal, ethical, and supportable with written evidence.
- Only use data that is either:
  - public and lawfully reusable,
  - provided directly by the customer with clear permission, or
  - created internally by the business.
- Do not automate actions that would violate platform terms, privacy expectations, or anti-spam rules.
- Keep an audit trail for every launch decision, including why an idea was approved or rejected.

---

## 2. Explicitly Prohibited
- Extracting email addresses from personal inboxes, sent mail, CC lists, or attached files for resale.
- Selling, brokering, renting, or swapping personal email lists.
- Scraping or exporting inbox data without the account owner's explicit opt-in for a defined service.
- Sending cold email, bulk outreach, or list uploads without a lawful basis and recipient consent where required.
- Buying or aggregating "lead" data from questionable sources.
- Routing business funds straight into a personal wallet or handle without bookkeeping, tax, and compliance controls.
- Treating leveraged trading as a Phase 1 revenue engine.

Any workflow that matches one of the items above is an automatic **REJECT**.

---

## 3. Approved Phase 1 Revenue Categories
- **Consent-based inbox-to-CRM hygiene service:** a customer connects their own inbox and authorizes extraction of contacts for deduping, tagging, and CRM cleanup.
- **AI listing and resale services:** create listings, improve media, and manage buyer communication for legitimate sellers.
- **Content products:** tutorials, operating playbooks, templates, prompts, and courses documenting the build journey.
- **Affiliate or partner distribution:** only for products with transparent disclosures and compliant acquisition channels.
- **Public-data research products:** market maps, industry trackers, and benchmarking reports built from lawful public sources.

---

## 4. Revenue Qualification Checklist
An idea must score well on all of the following before launch:
1. **Legality:** Is the activity lawful in the relevant jurisdictions?
2. **Consent:** Does the data subject or customer clearly authorize the workflow?
3. **Platform compliance:** Is the workflow compatible with source platform rules?
4. **Operational repeatability:** Can it be delivered consistently with automation plus limited review?
5. **Margin profile:** Is there a plausible path to positive unit economics?
6. **Reputational safety:** Would the workflow still be acceptable if publicly documented?

If legality or consent is weak, the idea is rejected even if the margin is attractive.

---

## 5. Finance and Treasury Rules
- Use a business ledger and a reviewable payout path.
- Record revenue source, costs, owner, and tax treatment for each program.
- Keep speculative trading separated from operating revenue.
- Do not automate custody, transfers, or deposits into personal payment accounts from this repository.

---

## 6. Data Handling Standard
- Minimize collection.
- Store only the fields needed to deliver the approved service.
- Define retention and deletion behavior before launch.
- Redact or avoid sensitive personal data unless strictly required and consented.
- Prefer aggregated or anonymized reporting where possible.

---

## 7. Operating Decision
- If an idea depends on unconsented inbox access or personal-data resale, mark it **REJECT**.
- If an idea can be reframed around customer consent and service delivery, mark it **REFRAME** and rescore it.
- Only **GO** ideas should proceed to implementation and testing.
