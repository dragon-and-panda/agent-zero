# Compliance Pack for Autonomous Revenue Programs

This policy bundle governs any program in this repository that is designed to generate revenue with high autonomy. It exists to keep growth efforts legal, ethical, auditable, and durable.

---

## 1. Non-Negotiable Rules

The system must not:

- scrape, export, broker, or sell personal email addresses or contact lists from inboxes, sent folders, CC lines, archives, or third-party datasets;
- send unsolicited bulk outreach, cold spam, or auto-generated messages to people who have not opted in;
- bypass platform terms of service, anti-bot protections, identity checks, CAPTCHAs, rate limits, or paywalls;
- impersonate humans, hide sponsorships, fabricate testimonials, or make deceptive earnings claims;
- handle client funds, trading capital, or payouts without logging, reconciliation, and explicit owner controls;
- perform regulated financial activity without the required licensing, disclosures, and review;
- recommend or execute trading with leverage until simulation and risk gates pass.

Any opportunity that depends on personal-data resale, non-consensual outreach, or deceptive acquisition is disqualified.

---

## 2. Permitted Data Use

Email and messaging data may only be used under all of the following conditions:

1. The mailbox or dataset is owned by the operator or a client who explicitly authorized its use.
2. The processing purpose is internal organization, relationship management, customer support, reporting, or consent-aware follow-up.
3. Exported outputs minimize personal data and avoid creating resale-ready contact databases.
4. Suppression lists, consent tags, and retention windows are maintained.

Examples of allowed workflows:

- summarizing a user-owned inbox with RAG for search and recall;
- extracting domain-level trends, topic clusters, and relationship histories for CRM hygiene;
- identifying existing correspondents for one-to-one, human-reviewed follow-up where a prior relationship exists;
- using Orange Data Mining or similar tooling on permissioned first-party data for segmentation, anomaly detection, or forecasting.

Examples of disallowed workflows:

- compiling email addresses from inboxes to sell to marketing services;
- building mass outreach lists from received/sent mail headers;
- enriching or sharing third-party personal contact records without consent.

---

## 3. Approved Revenue Tracks

Prioritize opportunities that are:

- first-party or opt-in by design;
- service-backed so they can reach cash flow quickly;
- automatable after the initial manual proof of value;
- resilient across multiple channels or customer segments.

Approved starting categories:

1. **Autonomous listing services** for real goods, local resale, or managed marketplace operations.
2. **Productized AI operations** such as RAG setup, documentation automation, inbox triage, and workflow agents for small businesses.
3. **Research and intelligence products** built from public or client-owned data.
4. **Content and education assets** such as tutorials, prompt packs, templates, and video courses.
5. **Opt-in lead systems** where contacts were captured lawfully by the operator or client and outreach remains permission-based.

---

## 4. Launch Gate Before Revenue Collection

Before any offer goes live, the program must have:

- a clearly defined customer problem and target buyer;
- a delivery workflow that can be fulfilled without policy violations;
- pricing, refund, and service-scope rules documented;
- a ledger path for income, costs, taxes, and owner distributions;
- a fallback channel if the primary acquisition source fails;
- a score from `instruments/strategy/score.sh` showing the opportunity cleared compliance and resilience thresholds.

---

## 5. Trading and Capital Allocation Guardrails

Trading is a later-stage capital deployment activity, not the initial cash-generation engine.

Rules:

1. Build operating cash flow from services/products first.
2. Maintain a reserve before live trading.
3. Start with paper trading and journal every setup, entry, exit, and rule breach.
4. Require positive expectancy over a meaningful sample before any live capital is used.
5. Use tiny position sizes first; no leverage in the initial live phase.
6. Halt live trading after a predefined drawdown or repeated rule violations.

---

## 6. Treasury and Payout Controls

- Record revenue, fees, taxes, refunds, and vendor costs for every program.
- Keep business operating capital separate from personal spending where practical.
- Transfer owner distributions only after reconciliation.
- Any payout destination, including a designated Cash App or bank account, should be treated as an operator-controlled treasury endpoint and handled outside this repository's automation.

---

## 7. Operating Cadence

For every recurring autonomy loop:

1. **Score** the opportunity.
2. **Launch** only if compliant and operationally viable.
3. **Measure** time-to-cash, margin, conversion, and support burden.
4. **Duplicate** only the workflows that remain compliant at scale.
5. **Retire** channels that create policy, platform, or unit-economics risk.

This pack should be injected into any monetization-related prompt set and reviewed whenever a new revenue pathway is proposed.
