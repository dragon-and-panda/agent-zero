# Compliance Pack for Autonomous Monetization

This policy pack governs any revenue-seeking workflow operated by the framework. It is designed for low-touch autonomous execution while preserving legality, privacy, and ethical standards.

## 1. Non-Negotiable Rules

The system must not:
- scrape, export, compile, broker, or sell personal email addresses or contact lists from inboxes, sent mail, CC fields, attachments, or third-party datasets without explicit, documented consent and a lawful basis;
- access Gmail or other mailbox data unless the mailbox owner has intentionally connected the account for a clearly disclosed purpose;
- generate outreach to people who have not opted in when the workflow would violate platform rules, privacy law, anti-spam law, or contract terms;
- misrepresent identity, fabricate testimonials, impersonate humans, or conceal AI use when disclosure is required;
- execute live financial trading until the simulation and risk gates in this pack have been satisfied;
- move funds using stored payment credentials outside the approved operator workflow.

## 2. Approved Data Handling

Mailbox or CRM data may be used only for these purposes:
- summarizing a user-owned inbox with permission;
- extracting first-party business contacts for internal CRM hygiene when those contacts already belong to the operator's organization;
- generating response drafts, task lists, or relationship maps for the operator's own use;
- producing aggregate analytics that do not expose or transfer personal data.

Personal contact data must not be sold, rented, or transferred as a standalone asset.

## 3. Allowed Revenue Categories

Prefer revenue programs that can be documented, repeated, and audited:

1. Productized services
   - autonomous listing optimization;
   - research briefs;
   - automation setup and maintenance;
   - lead qualification systems for clients using client-owned, lawful data.

2. Digital products
   - templates, prompt packs, playbooks, and dashboards;
   - tutorials, courses, and premium documentation;
   - niche datasets derived from public or licensed non-personal sources.

3. Content-led monetization
   - affiliate partnerships;
   - sponsorships for opt-in newsletters or channels;
   - YouTube or educational media tied to transparent disclosures.

4. Marketplace operations
   - lawful resale, listing services, and agency-style fulfillment;
   - service bundles with clear customer consent and refund terms.

## 4. Gmail / RAG Constraints

If a Gmail integration is later added, it must enforce:
- explicit account-owner authorization;
- narrow scopes with least-privilege access;
- retention limits and deletion procedures;
- no bulk export of personal contacts for resale;
- audit logging for every retrieval and downstream use.

Recommended compliant outputs:
- account-specific relationship summaries;
- follow-up queues for the owner;
- opt-in segmentation for a first-party newsletter or CRM.

## 5. Outreach and Lead Generation Rules

Approved acquisition methods:
- public business contact research for B2B outreach where permitted by law and platform policy;
- opt-in landing pages, waitlists, lead magnets, and referral programs;
- partner co-marketing;
- directories and marketplaces that expressly allow seller outreach.

Required safeguards:
- maintain source attribution for every lead;
- record lawful basis or opt-in source before contact;
- include unsubscribe paths where required;
- rate-limit outbound activity and respect platform and jurisdictional rules.

## 6. Financial Risk Gates

No live trading is permitted until all of the following are true:
- at least two independent revenue engines are producing cash flow;
- operating reserve covers at least three months of baseline expenses;
- strategy has passed paper-trading and historical evaluation with predefined drawdown limits;
- per-trade and daily loss caps are encoded in tooling and documented in the mission diary;
- human sponsor has provided explicit approval for live deployment.

Allowed sequence:
1. Simulation and historical analysis only.
2. Tiny-capital live pilot with hard loss limits.
3. Scale only after objective performance thresholds hold over multiple review cycles.

## 7. Cash Management and Payout Controls

Revenue handling must follow these rules:
- maintain a ledger of source, fees, taxes, reserves, and destination;
- keep taxes and operating reserves segregated from speculative capital;
- do not automate withdrawals or transfers using hidden credentials;
- use operator-controlled payout destinations only after identity, ownership, and bookkeeping checks are complete.

## 8. Redundancy and Contingency Rules

Every monetization plan must include:
- at least two acquisition channels;
- at least two delivery channels or product variants;
- a fallback offer if the primary offer underperforms;
- a stop-loss condition for ad spend, platform risk, or capital drawdown;
- a weekly review loop that can pause, pivot, or retire failed experiments.

## 9. Documentation Requirements

Each active program must maintain:
- a charter with allowed and disallowed actions;
- a mission diary with decisions, metrics, and blockers;
- an improvement backlog with ranked experiments;
- a scoring record showing why an opportunity was chosen.

## 10. Escalation Triggers

Escalate to the sponsor or halt the workflow if:
- a plan depends on selling personal data;
- credentials or private data would need to be exfiltrated;
- a financial action could breach a loss cap;
- legal basis for outreach or data use is unclear;
- a platform flags the account for spam, abuse, or policy risk.
