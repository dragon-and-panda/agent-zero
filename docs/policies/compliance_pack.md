# Compliance Pack for Autonomous Revenue Programs

This policy pack defines the minimum legal, ethical, and operational guardrails for any autonomous revenue workflow built in this repository.

---

## 1. Hard Prohibitions

The system must not:

- Harvest personal email addresses from Gmail, Google Workspace, or any other mailbox without explicit, documented authorization from the mailbox owner.
- Compile, broker, rent, or sell personal email lists gathered from received mail, sent mail, CC/BCC fields, attachments, or local files.
- Send spam, deceptive outreach, or messages to contacts who have not opted in or otherwise established a lawful basis for contact.
- Circumvent authentication, CAPTCHAs, rate limits, platform terms, or anti-abuse controls.
- Exfiltrate credentials, tokens, contact databases, or personal data into third-party datasets for resale.
- Automate transfers to personal payment handles or wallets without manual review, bookkeeping, and platform compliance checks.
- Begin live leveraged or speculative trading before a written risk policy, journaled paper-trading period, and hard loss limits are in place.

Any proposal containing these patterns is an automatic REJECT in intake review.

---

## 2. Allowed Data Sources

Allowed sources must be one of the following:

- First-party website leads collected through clear consent language.
- Existing CRM exports where the operator has documented ownership or processing rights.
- Customer-authorized mailbox or workspace data accessed for a service the customer requested, such as inbox cleanup or CRM hygiene.
- Licensed datasets with terms that explicitly allow the intended commercial use.
- Public business information gathered in ways that comply with site terms and local law.

Orange Data Mining or similar tools may be used only on allowed datasets. The tool is for analysis, segmentation, and deduplication, not for laundering unlawfully acquired data into a sales asset.

---

## 3. Approved Monetization Paths

Preferred revenue tracks:

1. Consent-based services
   - Inbox-to-CRM hygiene for customer-owned mailboxes
   - Lead qualification for opt-in inquiries
   - Listing automation and marketplace operations
2. Digital products
   - Templates, playbooks, prompt packs, courses, and internal tooling
3. Audience businesses
   - Newsletter, YouTube, education, and sponsorships built on original content
4. Compliant affiliate or referral revenue
   - Only with clear disclosure and accurate claims

Disallowed monetization paths:

- Personal data brokerage
- Spam-driven lead generation
- Credentialed scraping meant to resell contact information
- Guaranteed-return investment claims

---

## 4. Financial Risk Controls

- Default to services and products before trading or capital-intensive bets.
- Treat trading as a research program, not an immediate revenue source.
- Require paper trading, journaled hypotheses, position sizing rules, and maximum drawdown rules before any live deployment.
- Keep operating cash in auditable business accounts or approved processors first.
- Manual payout review is required before funds are moved to any personal destination.

---

## 5. Operational Controls

Every revenue workflow must maintain:

- A source registry showing where each dataset came from and why it is lawful to use.
- A consent ledger when personal contact data is involved.
- A suppression list for unsubscribes, opt-outs, and do-not-contact records.
- A decision log recording why an initiative was approved, held, or rejected.
- A service-specific contingency plan covering platform lockouts, refund events, and revenue concentration risk.

---

## 6. Escalation Triggers

Pause the workflow and require human review if any of the following occur:

- The initiative touches regulated financial activity, healthcare data, or minors' data.
- The data source cannot be traced to a clear owner or lawful license.
- The plan depends on avoiding terms of service or exploiting gray-area automation.
- A payout path lacks tax, accounting, or fraud controls.

---

## 7. Safe Replacements for Rejected Ideas

Replace unsafe ideas with adjacent lawful offers:

- Instead of selling email lists, offer consent-based CRM cleanup and segmentation for clients.
- Instead of scraping inboxes for resale, build an inbox-to-knowledge workflow for the mailbox owner.
- Instead of rushing into Forex, run a paper-trading lab and focus revenue generation on services and products first.
- Instead of auto-depositing to a personal handle, route payouts through a reviewed finance workflow.
