# Ethical Autonomous Financial System Blueprint

This blueprint translates an aggressive autonomy mission into a lawful, consent-first execution model that can run with low human touch inside Agent Zero.

It is designed for operators who want:
- autonomous tool generation,
- RAG-driven intelligence pipelines,
- scalable online revenue systems,
- disciplined capital allocation,
- and documented media/course output.

See also: `docs/policies/compliance_pack.md`.

---

## 1. Mission Translation

### Original intent (high-level)
- Build a self-sustaining online financial engine.
- Use agentic automation to gather intelligence and execute.
- Turn operations into repeatable content and training assets.

### Policy-safe interpretation
- Build a **consent-based audience and client acquisition engine**.
- Use RAG on first-party, authorized datasets only.
- Monetize through legal digital products/services rather than selling personal data.
- Deploy profits using strict trading risk controls and accounting discipline.

---

## 2. Agentic Framework Design

### 2.1 Core agent roles
- **Apex Orchestrator:** sets weekly objectives and routes tasks.
- **Data Steward Agent:** enforces data provenance and consent checks.
- **Growth Operator Agent:** runs lawful acquisition and conversion experiments.
- **Revenue Architect Agent:** builds offers, pricing ladders, and funnels.
- **Risk Controller Agent:** enforces trading/risk limits and kill switches.
- **Content Producer Agent:** turns each sprint into docs, scripts, and media assets.

### 2.2 Required controls
- All agents must import the compliance pack before execution.
- Any task touching personal data must include source + consent metadata.
- High-risk actions (payments, trading, bulk outreach) require dual-check prompts.

---

## 3. Data Extraction and RAG (Compliant Path)

### 3.1 Gmail/communication ingestion
- Allow ingestion only from operator-owned accounts or explicit delegated access.
- Normalize records into:
  - `contact_email`
  - `source_type` (received/sent/cc/other)
  - `consent_status`
  - `last_interaction_at`
  - `notes`

### 3.2 RAG usage
- Use retrieval to:
  - summarize relationship context,
  - classify conversation intent,
  - prioritize follow-up opportunities.
- Never use RAG outputs to generate unsolicited mass outreach.

### 3.3 Data operations tooling
- Use Orange Data Mining or equivalent for segmentation, dedupe, and scoring.
- Enforce suppression lists and auto-removal workflows.
- Keep immutable provenance logs for every imported record.

## 4. Monetization Phase 1 (Legal, Fast-to-Launch)

1. **Niche service offers:** automation setup, workflow audits, or implementation retainers.
2. **Affiliate + partner funnels:** audience-first content with disclosed monetization.
3. **Digital products:** templates, prompt packs, mini-courses, and playbooks.
4. **Sponsored newsletter/media:** only with opted-in subscribers.

### Weekly loop
1. Pick one audience/problem segment.
2. Publish one value asset (post, short video, teardown, or case study).
3. Run one offer experiment (price, framing, bonus).
4. Measure CAC, conversion, refund/churn, and net cashflow.
5. Write outcomes to mission diary and adjust.

---

## 5. Financial Expansion (Post-Phase 1)

### 5.1 Trading launch protocol
- Stage 1: paper trading only.
- Stage 2: micro-size live deployment.
- Stage 3: scale only after statistically significant performance.

### 5.2 Mandatory risk policy
- Fixed risk per trade.
- Maximum daily and weekly drawdown.
- Hard stop-loss and no-override kill switch.
- Strategy journal with pre/post-trade rationale.

### 5.3 Performance governance
- Compare live performance to backtest assumptions.
- Disable strategies that exceed variance thresholds.
- Keep capital allocation dynamic and evidence-based.

---

## 6. Cashflow Operations

- Route business revenue through traceable accounting first.
- Schedule periodic owner draws/transfers to approved payout channels.
- Reconcile payout records against invoices and ledger entries.
- Produce weekly financial snapshots (cash in, cash out, retained capital, owner draw).

## 7. Content and IP Engine

### 7.1 Capture protocol
- Every sprint outputs:
  - mission log update,
  - what changed/why,
  - metrics impacted,
  - next hypothesis.

### 7.2 Productize the journey
- Compile SOPs into a tutorial/course format.
- Convert modules into short and long-form video scripts.
- Use an anthropomorphic narrator character for continuity and brand storytelling.

### 7.3 Narrative arc template
1. Constraint and challenge.
2. System design decision.
3. Experiment and metric outcome.
4. Failure/recovery and improvement.
5. Next mission toward the long-term vision.

---

## 8. 30-60-90 Day Rollout

### Days 0-30
- Stand up compliance controls and data schema.
- Launch one consent-based lead capture pipeline.
- Publish first public case-study content.

### Days 31-60
- Ship two monetization channels (service + digital product).
- Add RAG-driven segmentation and follow-up prioritization.
- Implement trading simulator and risk dashboards.

### Days 61-90
- Optimize conversion loops and retention sequences.
- Begin micro-size live trading under strict risk caps.
- Publish course beta and media series cadence.

---

## 9. Success Metrics

- **Compliance:** zero prohibited data events.
- **Growth:** net-new opt-in contacts per week.
- **Revenue:** monthly recurring and one-time revenue mix.
- **Efficiency:** agent-hours saved and task cycle time.
- **Capital:** risk-adjusted return and drawdown stability.
- **Content:** publish cadence and tutorial completion progress.

This keeps autonomy high while maintaining legal, ethical, and operational resilience.
